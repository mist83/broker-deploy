Text content (placeholder copy, phrase banks, any creative writing in a product) must not have "AI stinky stink."

Operator quote: "it can have zombie stink on it, because zombies are dead, and they stink, but not AI stinky stink."

**Why:** The operator builds products for a taste-sensitive audience. Generic AI voice (overpolished, sycophantic, parallel-structure-heavy, "delve into the tapestry of") undermines credibility and makes the product feel disposable.

**How to apply:**
- Specific details over generic platitudes ("bowling alley" not "facility")
- Dry humor, not trying-too-hard jokes
- Varied sentence structures — avoid parallel construction patterns
- Weird specificity ("the clock on the microwave still blinked twelve")
- Incomplete thoughts and trailing observations
- Things funny because recognizably true, not because constructed to be clever
- No "brains" in zombie content — gets old in ten seconds
- If a line could appear in any AI-generated listicle, cut it
