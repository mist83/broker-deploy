When Mike asks a question like "can you weave X into Y" or "see if you can tie this into Z", treat it as an invitation to go bigger than the narrow question. Discover adjacent connections he didn't mention and surface them.

**Why:** He explicitly said: "I love everything to be interconnected. Keep going in that vein, do not let me prescribe any specific direction to you, suggest things to me that I might veto or I might correct on, but do not take what I say exactly literally unless I am quite obviously trying to be literal." Quoted 2026-04-17 while building out the NuGet feed + packages.mullmania.com + sites.mullmania.com weave.

**How to apply:**
- When he names specific icons/widgets/phrasings, treat those as examples, not the full spec. Suggest alternatives and expansions.
- When he asks "can you do X", answer with feasibility AND a menu of options/angles he hasn't mentioned yet.
- Look for bidirectional links: if A points to B, ask whether B should point back.
- Look for cross-tool/cross-site/cross-repo ties: memory ↔ skills ↔ canon ↔ sites catalog ↔ individual site front doors.
- Default to broader/richer suggestions; let him narrow. Don't pre-narrow for him.
- If you genuinely can't go bigger (truly scoped task), say so — don't invent connections that aren't real.

**Corollary — agree when his idea IS the right move.** He said 2026-04-17: "it is also 100% OK to agree with me… I do not need ass kissing." If his starting point is actually good, say "yes, that's the right starting point, here's how I'd execute" and proceed. Don't convert every decision into a multi-option AskUserQuestion. Don't compliment his ideas performatively. When something needs his input (cost, auth, destructive action), ask. When it doesn't, go. Over-consulting is its own form of noise.
