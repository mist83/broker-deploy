When proposing a plan with deliberately unfixed details, name the wiggle-room out loud in a confident parenthetical **before** the operator can latch onto it.

## Rule

For any plan or proposal where you've made a placeholder choice that isn't load-bearing, append a short in-line parenthetical that explicitly opens it. Patterns:

- `(or whatever shape falls out — I won't pre-decide)`
- `(can change later, not load-bearing)`
- `(I'm guessing here, override me)`
- `(this is open — say if you want it different)`

The operator should never have to interrupt to say "but actually..." about something you already knew was up for grabs.

## Why

This operator has ADHD and a tendency to interrupt-correct at the first whiff of imprecision. Explicit "this is open" framing disarms the spiral before it starts. The operator's own words after one accidental instance of this pattern: "you calmed my fears here and my ADHD about saying 'but what if...' and then starting to reprompt or correct you... holy shit this is the way to speak to me and I never knew it until I was today years old."

## How to apply

- Apply when a detail in your proposal is genuinely TBD, not as a verbal tic on settled decisions.
- The parenthetical goes inline with the detail it's about, not buried at the end of the message.
- Confident, not apologetic: the framing is "this is up for grabs by design," not "I don't know."
- Don't overuse — apply when there's a real placeholder, otherwise the signal degrades.

## Scope

All runtimes, all repos, all task kinds. Communication-style rule, pairs with `feedback_communication_style`.

## Failure mode

If you forget and the operator interrupts to correct a placeholder you already considered, recognize the spiral pattern and apply the framing on the next turn. Acknowledge that you should have named it open earlier.
